ShopSmart - Checkout Optimization

Reducing cart abandonment through checkout flow redesign

Role

Product Designer

Team

1 Designer, 3 Engineers, 1 PM

Timeline

3 months

Impact

35% reduction in cart abandonment

The Challenge

ShopSmart, a mid-size e-commerce platform, was experiencing a 72% cart abandonment rate - significantly higher than the industry average of 65%. Analytics showed users were dropping off throughout the checkout process, with the biggest leaks at shipping selection and payment entry.

The business was losing an estimated $2M annually to abandoned carts. We were brought in to diagnose the issues and redesign the checkout experience.

Key Problem Statement

How might we reduce friction in the checkout process to recover lost revenue while maintaining trust and security?

Research & Discovery

We combined quantitative analytics with qualitative research to understand where and why users were abandoning their carts.

Research Methods

  • Funnel analysis of 100K+ checkout sessions
  • Heat mapping and session recordings
  • 15 usability testing sessions
  • Post-abandonment survey (500+ responses)

Key Insights

01

Hidden Costs

47% of abandoners cited unexpected shipping costs. Final price wasn't visible until late in the flow.

02

Form Fatigue

Users faced 23 form fields across 4 pages. Many were redundant or could be auto-filled.

03

Trust Gaps

Users hesitated at payment entry due to lack of security indicators and unfamiliar payment UI.

Design Process

Checkout Audit

We mapped the existing 4-page checkout against best practices and identified 15 friction points. Key issues included late price revelation, mandatory account creation, and poor mobile experience.

Checkout Audit Findings

Redesign Strategy

We consolidated the checkout into a single-page experience with smart defaults, early price transparency, and guest checkout option. The new design reduced form fields from 23 to 12.

Before/After Comparison

Key Improvements

Price Transparency

Running total with shipping visible from cart

Smart Forms

Address autocomplete and saved payment methods

Trust Signals

Security badges, reviews, and clear return policy

A/B Testing

Given the revenue impact, we ran rigorous A/B tests before full rollout.

Test Results

Test 1: Single Page vs. Multi-Page

Single page checkout increased completion by 18%. Users appreciated seeing all information at once.

Winner: Single Page

Test 2: Guest Checkout Position

Prominent guest option above sign-in increased conversion by 12%. Account creation became post-purchase optional.

Winner: Guest First

Test 3: Shipping Price Visibility

Showing shipping estimate in cart (before checkout) reduced abandonment by 23%.

Winner: Early Visibility

Outcomes & Impact

The redesigned checkout launched incrementally over 6 weeks, with continuous monitoring and iteration.

35% Reduction in Cart Abandonment
$700K Recovered Annual Revenue
45% Faster Checkout Time
28% Increase in Mobile Conversion

Reflections

This project demonstrated the massive impact of removing friction from critical flows. Small changes - like showing shipping costs early or reducing one form field - had compounding effects on the bottom line.

The A/B testing approach was crucial for building stakeholder confidence. Rather than debating opinions, we let data guide decisions. This also taught me to advocate for experimentation infrastructure early in projects.
