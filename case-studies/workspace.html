WorkSpace - Team Collaboration

Building a collaboration platform from 0 to 1 for enterprise teams

Role

Product Designer

Team

3 Designers, 8 Engineers, 2 PMs

Timeline

8 months

Impact

50K+ users across 12 countries

The Challenge

Enterprise teams were struggling with tool fragmentation. Communication happened in one app, documents lived in another, and project tracking was scattered across spreadsheets. The lack of integration led to context-switching fatigue and lost information.

We were tasked with building a unified collaboration platform that could replace 5+ tools while remaining simple enough for non-technical users.

Key Problem Statement

How might we create a single workspace that unifies communication, documentation, and project management without overwhelming users with complexity?

Research & Discovery

Given the complexity of this 0-to-1 product, we invested heavily in understanding the landscape and user needs.

Research Methods

  • 30+ interviews with enterprise employees across roles
  • Workflow shadowing sessions with 5 teams
  • Tool audit analyzing 20+ existing solutions
  • Stakeholder workshops to align on vision

Key Insights

01

Context is King

Users spent 30% of their time just finding information and context across different tools.

02

Flexibility vs. Structure

Different teams needed different workflows - rigid systems failed, but too much flexibility led to chaos.

03

Adoption Barriers

Previous tool migrations failed due to learning curves. New tools needed to feel instantly familiar.

Design Process

Product Strategy

We defined a modular architecture where teams could adopt features incrementally. Start with chat, add documents when ready, then project tracking - all interconnected but independently useful.

Product Strategy Diagram

Design System

We built a comprehensive design system from day one, enabling rapid iteration while maintaining consistency across a complex product surface.

Design System Components

Key Features

Unified Inbox

All messages, mentions, and tasks in one filterable view

Smart Documents

Docs that connect to tasks and conversations

Flexible Projects

Kanban, list, or timeline views with custom fields

Usability Testing

We ran 4 rounds of usability testing throughout development, iterating based on real user feedback.

Key Iterations

Navigation Redesign

Initial sidebar was too deep. We flattened to 2 levels with a powerful search overlay, reducing navigation time by 60%.

Onboarding Flow

First version threw users into empty workspace. We added progressive onboarding with sample content and guided tours.

Notification Management

Users felt overwhelmed. We implemented smart defaults and granular controls with "focus modes" for deep work.

Outcomes & Impact

WorkSpace launched in 2023 and has grown to serve thousands of enterprise teams worldwide.

50K+ Active Users
12 Countries
4.5 G2 Rating
85% Tool Consolidation Rate

Reflections

Building a 0-to-1 product taught me the importance of saying no. With unlimited possibilities, the hardest decisions were about what NOT to build. We succeeded by focusing relentlessly on core workflows rather than feature parity with competitors.

The modular approach was crucial - it allowed teams to adopt at their own pace and validated our design decisions incrementally rather than betting everything on a big bang launch.
