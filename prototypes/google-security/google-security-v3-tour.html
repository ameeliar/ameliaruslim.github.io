How to sign in with security keys

After entering your password, you'll need to use your key to complete the sign-in. Click the images to see how.

To sign in with your security key using USB

After you enter your email address and password to sign in, you'll be prompted to connect your security key.

  1. Insert your security key into a USB port on your computer.
  2. If asked, tap the button or gold disc on your key.
  3. You'll see a confirmation once your identity is verified.

To sign in with your security key using NFC

After you enter your email address and password to sign in, you'll be prompted to connect your security key.

  1. Hold your security key flat against the back of your phone, at the center of the top edge.
  2. You'll see a checkmark on your phone's screen when it connects.

To sign in with your security key using Bluetooth

After you enter your email address and password to sign in, you'll be prompted to connect your security key via Bluetooth.

  1. Turn on your security key and make sure Bluetooth is enabled on your device.
  2. Follow the on-screen instructions to pair your key if it's not already paired.
  3. Press the button on your security key when prompted.

To sign in using your phone's built-in security key

Your phone has a built-in security key that can be used as a second step to verify your identity when signing in.

  1. Make sure Bluetooth is turned on for both your phone and the device you're signing in on.
  2. On the device you're signing in on, follow the on-screen instructions.
  3. Check the notification on your phone and confirm the sign-in.
